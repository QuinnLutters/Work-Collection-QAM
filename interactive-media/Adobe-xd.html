Prototying in Adobe XD

The first thing I’ve done to get an idea on how I would like my portfolio to look was by searching the web for inspiration, I used awwwards.com which I greatly recommend if you need any inspiration for your websites. Eventually I settled on a design using pastel colors with a warm and soothing feel to it. Then I started to put something on paper and eventually I had a loose sketch of what I wanted, which meant that I could start on making a higher fidelity prototype. This was my first time using Adobe XD after I’ve been using figma most of the time, I started with looking for different color palettes and trying them out on my homepage. After I decided to use the palette called Odin’s Crow I made all of my pages and I tried multiple kinds of layouts for my content pages. With each page I tried to take up as much space as I could, while still allowing each element enough white space to allow it to “breathe”.
