Making a style guide

In order to try to better my skills in InDesign I decided to make a style guide, which also gives me a guideline on how people should use my products. I made three different sections in my style guide, one for the color palette, one for typography and one for my logo. I had a hard time figuring out how to change the background color in InDesign, but it turns out that you can’t change the actual background, but instead you can put a big rectangle behind everything and lock it in place. Having the ability to make classes you can apply to text is really helpful, since it adds consistency and you don’t have to change each individual thing every time. After finishing my style guide I think that I have decent skills in InDesign and that I’m able to make layouts and photo books in InDesign with relative ease
